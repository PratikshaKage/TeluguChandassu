Telugu Chandassu

Telugu Chandassu, also known as Telugu prosody or Telugu chandas, is a system of rules that governs the structure and rhythm of poetry in the Telugu language. It is an ancient and complex system that has been used for centuries to create classical Telugu poetry.

Telugu Chandassu is based on the principles of syllabic length, stress, and rhyme. The length of a syllable is classified into two types - laghu (short) and guru (long). The stress of a syllable is determined by its position within a word or line of poetry. The rhyme, or chanda, is determined by the pattern of syllabic length and stress within a line of poetry.
